Introduction

Methods

Weather radar data: bird vector speed and density
Bird density, orientation and speed for 37 weather radars in western Europe are downloaded from the ENRAM repository (ref) and processed as explained in Nussbaumer (2020) (https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.10.13.321844). The final dataset consisting of bird density (ρ) [bird/km^3], flight speed following the east-west component (u) and south-north component (v) [m/s] at 15min resolutions, 200m bin altitude (0-5km) for each radar are available on zenodo https://zenodo.org/record/3610185.
Climate Reanalaysis: Wind vector speed at pressure level
The U (parmID=131) and V (parmID=132) component of wind are downloaed from the ERA5 reanalysis (ref) at pressure level (from 1000hPa to 550hPa), downloaded at the maximal resoluation (hourly and 0.25°x0.25°). Both components (U and V) are linearly interpolated (time-space 4D) at each datapoint of the weather radar data.

v_g(:,:,i_d) = sqrt(dc(i_d).u2.^2 + dc(i_d).v2.^2);
% air speed
v_a(:,:,i_d) = sqrt((dc(i_d).u2-dc(i_d).windu).^2 +(dc(i_d).v2-dc(i_d).windv).^2);
v_w (:,:,i_d) = sqrt(dc(i_d).windu.^2 + dc(i_d).windv.^2);
dens(:,:,i_d) = dc(i_d).dens4;
end

function [e,h,M,S] = histvdens(v,dens,bin)
if ~exist('bin','var'), bin = 200; end
id=~isnan(v)&~isnan(dens);
[Y,edge] = discretize(v(id),bin);
[G,ID] = findgroups(Y);
h=zeros(numel(edge)-1,1);
h(ID) = splitapply(@sum, dens(id), G);
e = 0.5 * (edge(1:end-1) + edge(2:end));
M = nansum(v(id).*dens(id))/nansum(dens(id));
S = nanstd(v(id & dens>0),dens(id & dens>0));
end
